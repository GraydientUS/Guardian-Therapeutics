    Aptamers

    • Aptamers are short nucleic acids that bind to a specific target molecule
    • The word aptamer comes from the Latin aptus meaning fit
    • At the molecular level, aptamers bind to their target site through electrostatic interactions, hydrophobic interactions, and their complementary shapes
    • Aptamers have been discovered that are specific to small molecules, proteins, nucleic acids, cells, tissues and organisms
